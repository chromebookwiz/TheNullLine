At the 1 nm scale, the Casimir pressure is enormous — over a thousand atmospheres. This is real physics. The challenge is fabricating and maintaining 1 nm gaps over macroscopic areas, and preventing the surfaces from snapping together (the Casimir force is attractive for parallel plates).

**Casimir repulsion [ESTABLISHED in specific configurations]:** Lifshitz theory predicts that the Casimir force becomes repulsive when two different materials are separated by a fluid with intermediate dielectric properties. Specifically, if material A has permittivity ε_A, the fluid has ε_fluid, and material B has ε_B, and ε_A > ε_fluid > ε_B (at imaginary frequency), the force is repulsive. This was experimentally confirmed by Munday, Capasso & Parsegian (Nature 2009) for gold-silica across bromobenzene.

**For the disc:** Casimir repulsion enables frictionless bearings and self-spacing structural members. Two surfaces that repel each other via the Casimir force maintain a stable equilibrium gap without mechanical contact.

## VII. THE PASSENGER EXPERIENCE

### VII.1 Boarding

The disc sits on its landing gear, rim at waist height. A section of the rim (60° arc) hinges downward, becoming a ramp. The cabin floor is at knee height — you step up one low step and duck slightly (1.0 m headroom). The ramp closes and seals (the cabin is pressurised to 0.05 atm overpressure for dust and rain exclusion, not for high-altitude flight — the disc operates below 3,000 m / 10,000 ft in standard configuration).

### VII.2 Controls

The pilot's Null Cylinder sits in a dock on the centre console (the "Helm" dock, same as the car dock but with the disc's additional flight-command interface). The holographic canopy activates, showing the full-transparency view. Flight controls:

- **Voice:** "Disc, take off." "Disc, fly to 123 Main Street." "Disc, hover here." "Disc, land." The disc handles all flight dynamics autonomously. The pilot specifies destinations, not stick-and-rudder inputs.
- **Gesture (Null Glove):** For manual flight override — point where you want to go; the disc moves that direction. Raise your hand = ascend. Lower = descend. Fist = hover/stop. Two-hand rotate = yaw. This is intuitive spatial control — you point and the disc follows.
- **Grip ring scroll:** The Null Cylinder's pop-out ring, when in the Helm dock, controls altitude (scroll up = ascend, scroll down = descend) for fine vertical positioning during landing or precision hover.

### VII.3 Flight Modes

| Mode | Pilot input | Disc behaviour |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **Autonomous** | Destination only | Full self-flight: takeoff, route, obstacle avoidance, landing |
| **Guided** | Continuous direction input via gesture | Disc maintains altitude and stability; pilot controls direction and speed |
| **Hover** | None (or voice: "hover here") | Station-keeping at current position, ±10 cm accuracy (GPS + LiDAR) |
| **Follow** | Follows a person, vehicle, or path | Autonomous tracking, user-set distance and altitude |
| **Formation** | Multiple discs coordinate | Swarm flight for multi-disc operations (cargo, display, survey) |

### VII.4 Safety

- **Redundant propulsion:** 12 rim fans + 1 central fan = 13 independent lift sources. The disc can lose any 3 rim fans and the central fan can lose 50% power and still land safely (controlled descent to nearest safe surface).
- **Ballistic parachute:** A solid-rocket-deployed parachute (BRS Aerospace type) in the upper hull. Activated by pilot command or automatically by the avionics if total propulsion loss is detected. Lowers the disc to the ground at <7 m/s (survivable impact for occupants in seats with 5-point harness). Deployment altitude: effective above 30 m AGL.
- **Autorotation analogue:** If all electric power is lost (battery failure), the central fan's motor can be mechanically decoupled, allowing the fan to windmill freely. The descending disc's airflow through the fan generates enough drag to limit descent rate to ~15 m/s — not comfortable but survivable with harness.
- **Geofencing:** The avionics enforce no-fly zones (airports, military installations, restricted areas) loaded from aviation databases and updated via 5G. The disc physically will not fly into a restricted zone — the flight controller refuses the trajectory.

### IX.2 Experimental Protocol

1. **Baseline:** Measure Casimir force on the E₈ array with all spheres detuned from Riemann zeros (randomised radii). Record for 24 hours. This establishes the standard Casimir force baseline.

2. **Resonance scan:** Systematically tune sphere radii (via thermal expansion — a 0.1 K temperature change shifts R by ~0.01 nm for CaF₂) to bring modes into alignment with successive Riemann zeros γ₁, γ₂, γ₃, ... Record force as a function of mode-zero alignment. Look for anomalous enhancement.

3. **Symmetry test:** Measure force directionality. Rotate the E₈ array through 360° (the array mount is on a precision rotation stage). If the force has a directional component, it will show E₈ symmetry (peaks every 360°/240 = 1.5° in the full 8D projection, or specific high-symmetry angles in the 3D physical mounting).

4. **Null test:** Repeat with the array topology scrambled (random coupling, not E₈). If the effect (if any) disappears when E₈ symmetry is broken, this confirms the geometric origin.

### IX.3 What We Learn Either Way

**If no anomaly is found (most likely):** We have the highest-precision measurement of the Casimir effect in a complex multi-body geometry — publishable, valuable physics. We confirm that E₈ geometry does not produce anomalous vacuum forces, closing a speculative direction.

**If a small anomaly is found:** We have discovered a new Casimir effect in topologically structured cavity arrays. This is a major physics result regardless of whether it leads to propulsion. It would confirm a connection between the Riemann zero spectrum and physical vacuum forces — a result that would electrify both physics and mathematics.

**If a directional anomaly is found:** This would be revolutionary — a violation of the assumed isotropy of the quantum vacuum in the presence of specific geometric boundary conditions. It would not immediately yield propulsion (the forces would be tiny), but it would establish the physical reality of vacuum force asymmetry, opening a new field of vacuum engineering.

## X. THE LONG VIEW

The Null Disc's three tiers represent three timescales:

**Now (Tier 1):** A flying disc with holographic display, photonic avionics, and electric propulsion. A spectacular personal air vehicle that demonstrates the Null ecosystem's capabilities in a form factor that captures the imagination. Flies on batteries and fans. Real and buildable.

**Near future (Tier 2):** The same disc with Casimir-engineered structures — frictionless bearings, shape-shifting airframe, metamaterial skin. Requires Null Forge fabrication of atomically precise surfaces at scale. Buildable in 10–15 years with the Forge technology described in the companion specification.

**Far future or never (Tier 3):** The disc as a vacuum energy research platform, testing whether the null framework's deepest mathematical suggestion — that the geometry of the Riemann zeros has physical consequences for the quantum vacuum — is real. If it is, the Null Disc becomes the prototype for propellantless flight. If it isn't, the disc remains a Tier 1/2 vehicle with an interesting physics experiment on board.

The honesty of this design is its strength. Every tier delivers value. Tier 1 alone justifies the vehicle. Tier 2 makes it extraordinary. Tier 3 is a dream with a rigorous experimental protocol attached — the kind of dream that occasionally turns out to be real, and that always produces good science when tested properly.
