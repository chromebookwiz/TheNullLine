## PART ONE: THE THREE MODES

The Null Thread operates in three distinct modes, each building on the last:

**Mode 1 — RECORD: Capture a lived experience as a replayable sensory stream.**
You walk through a forest, eat a meal, perform surgery, fly a Null Hover through a storm. The Thread records everything your body experienced: what you saw, heard, felt on your skin, smelled, tasted, and what your body was doing (heart rate, breathing, muscle tension, neurochemical state). The recording is a complete experiential file — a Thread — that someone else can play back and live through your body.

**Mode 2 — WEAVE: Edit, compose, and annotate Threads for education and storytelling.**
A master surgeon's Thread of a perfect operation is annotated with explanations at each step. A historian's Thread of walking through an ancient Roman street (reconstructed from archaeological data) is composed from fictional sensory elements. A musician's Thread of performing a concerto includes the proprioceptive sensation of their fingers on the strings. Threads are edited, layered, and combined into structured experiential curricula.

**Mode 3 — DREAM: Generate and enter a fully fictional immersive world in real time.**
The Null Loom designs a complete sensory environment from a description ("Renaissance Florence, spring, Ponte Vecchio at sunset"). The Null Cylinder projects the holographic visual and audio. The Null Cortex delivers the somatic experience (warm Italian air, the smell of leather and spices via olfactory nerve stimulation, the feel of cobblestones through proprioceptive mapping). The user enters the world and moves through it. The world responds to their actions in real time — the Loom generates new sensory content as the user explores, guided by the rules and history of the fictional setting.

## PART THREE: PLAYBACK — LIVING SOMEONE ELSE'S EXPERIENCE

### 3.1 The Playback System

Playback uses the same hardware as the Bridge receiver: the Null Cortex fleet delivers somatic stimuli, the Null Cylinder projects the holographic environment, and the Orbit OS Bridge Protocol translates the recorded state into the player's body.

**But playback has a critical difference from the Bridge: the somatic content is richer and the proprioceptive channel is active.**

In a live Bridge, proprioception (body position and movement) is not shared — you feel the other person's temperature and heartbeat, but your body stays in your own posture. In Thread playback, proprioception is optionally active: the playback system can guide the player's body through the movements the recorder made.

**Proprioceptive playback works through the Cortex nerve cuff + fleet coordination:**

1. The motor planning channel from the Thread provides the intended movement sequence (e.g., "right hand moves forward 15 cm, grasps, rotates 30° clockwise" — a surgical suturing motion).

2. The Cortex chip translates this into nerve cuff stimulation patterns that produce the corresponding proprioceptive sensation — the player feels their hand moving, grasping, and rotating, even if their actual hand is at rest.

3. **Crucially: the player's actual hand does NOT move.** The Cortex does not stimulate motor neurons (efferent fibres). It stimulates sensory neurons (afferent fibres) that produce the proprioceptive illusion of movement. The player feels the motion without performing it. This is identical to the phantom limb phenomenon — the brain perceives limb position from proprioceptive nerve signals, and those signals can be artificially generated.

4. If the player wants to physically perform the movement (in training mode — learning a surgical technique by doing it), they can opt into motor guidance: the nerve cuff provides gentle haptic cues (vibration on the side they should move toward, pressure when they should stop) that guide their actual hand through the recorded motion. This is not forcing the hand — it's coaching it, the way a dance teacher guides a student's arms.

## PART FOUR: MODE 2 — WEAVE (EDITING AND COMPOSITION)

### 4.1 The Weaving Interface

The Null Loom + Null Cylinder + Null Glove form the Weaving workstation. The Weaver (human or Archon-tier AI) works with Threads the way a film editor works with footage — but in full sensory dimensions.

**The holographic editing workspace:**

The Cylinder projects the Thread as a timeline — a horizontal ribbon of time floating in front of the Weaver. The ribbon is colour-coded by sensory intensity (bright = high sensation, dim = quiet). The Weaver can:

- **Scrub:** Orbit gesture scrolls through the timeline. The somatic playback tracks the scrub position — the Weaver feels the Thread at any point by scrolling to it.
- **Cut:** Pinch at two points to isolate a segment. Collapse gesture to delete. Spread to expand (time-stretch).
- **Layer:** Place one Thread above another on the timeline. The somatic channels blend (weighted average, user-adjustable). This allows compositing: a surgeon's hand movements from one Thread layered onto a different patient's anatomy from another Thread, creating a training Thread for a novel situation.
- **Annotate:** Voice or text annotations appear as floating markers above the timeline. In playback, annotations appear as a gentle holographic text overlay and optional voice narration (non-somatic — the narration is heard but not felt, preserving the immersion of the underlying Thread).
- **Branch:** Create interactive branch points where the player chooses between alternative continuations. The timeline forks into two or more ribbons. At playback, the player makes a choice (voice, gesture, or body movement) and the Thread follows that branch.

### 4.2 Composed Threads: Building Experiences That Never Happened

The most powerful Weaving operation: composing a Thread from scratch, using the Null Loom's constructive interference design engine to generate sensory content that no one has ever experienced.

**Inputs to the Loom:**
- A description: "Walking through Rome in 44 BC, the day before the assassination of Caesar. Forum Romanum, midday, spring."
- Historical data: archaeological floor plans, material analyses, climate reconstructions, contemporary textual descriptions (Cicero's letters, Suetonius)
- Physical models: the Loom's physics engine computes: sunlight angle and intensity for that date and latitude, air temperature (~18°C in Rome in mid-March), wind patterns in the Forum's canyon geometry, the acoustic signature of a marble-and-travertine space, the olfactory profile of a Roman market (baking bread, olive oil, animal dung, incense, unwashed humanity, marble dust)

**Loom output:** A complete Thread — visual, audio, somatic, olfactory, proprioceptive — of walking through the reconstructed Forum. The cobblestones are uneven under your feet (proprioceptive channel, derived from archaeological pavement surveys). The sun is warm on the left side of your face (thermal, computed from solar angle). The air smells of bread and smoke (olfactory, composed from the Loom's chemical library). You hear Latin spoken in the cadences reconstructed by historical linguists (audio, synthesised). A vendor's hand brushes yours as you pass his stall (tactile, fleeting, fabricated but physically plausible).

**Accuracy labelling:** Every composed Thread carries a fidelity tag:

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| **Recorded** | Real experience captured from a living person. Unedited. |
| **Edited** | Real experience with cuts, annotations, or time adjustments. Core sensation is authentic. |

**The fidelity tag is always visible during playback** — a subtle but permanent indicator in the player's peripheral vision. This is non-negotiable. The user must always know whether what they're experiencing is real or composed. Chesed insists: "The power to fabricate experience is the power to fabricate memory. If people cannot distinguish lived truth from constructed fiction, the concept of truth dissolves. The tag is the line."

### 5.1 The Architecture of a Dream

Dream mode is the full synthesis: the Null Loom generates a complete world in real time, the Null Cylinder projects it holographically, and the Null Cortex delivers the somatic experience. The user enters the world and acts within it. The world responds.

**The computational pipeline:**

1. **World definition:** A pre-authored world template — the setting, physics, characters, narrative rules, and aesthetic — is loaded into the Loom. This is authored by a human creator (writer, game designer, educator, historian) using the Weaving interface, or generated by a Magus (Sophia for scientific simulations, Chesed for empathy-training scenarios, Themis for emergency preparedness drills).

2. **Scene generation (Loom Weaver lobe):** The Loom continuously generates the sensory environment around the user — visual scene, spatial audio, ambient somatic state — 120 times per second (matching the holographic display refresh rate). The Loom's constructive interference design engine is repurposed: instead of designing a physical object that satisfies constraints, it designs a sensory experience that satisfies narrative and physical constraints. "The user has entered a medieval smithy. Generate: visual (fire, anvil, tools, smoke), audio (hammer on metal, bellows, crackling fire), thermal (intense radiant heat from the forge, cool draft from the door), olfactory (hot iron, charcoal, sweat), proprioceptive (the floor is hard stone, slightly gritty)."

3. **Interaction handling (Loom Judge lobe):** When the user acts (speaks, gestures, moves, picks up a virtual object), the Judge evaluates the action against the world's rules. "The user reached for the hammer. The hammer has mass 1.5 kg. The user's proprioceptive channel should feel the weight when they lift it. The forge fire illuminates the hammer's surface — update the visual reflection. The smith NPC observes the user picking up his hammer — what does the smith do?"

4. **NPC generation (Loom Listener lobe):** Non-player characters are generated in real time by the Listener — the same architecture that understands human intent is repurposed to generate human-like behaviour. The Listener models each NPC's personality, emotional state, and goals as a Design Intent Vector in E₈ space, and generates their actions and dialogue by the same constructive interference process that generates designs. An NPC is, computationally, a continuously generated "design" whose constraints are "behave consistently with this personality in this situation."

5. **Delivery:** Visual → Cylinder holographic projection (360° canopy for Null Disc users; 180° forward dome for Cylinder users; or a dedicated Dream Room — a 3 m sphere of holographic panels that surrounds the user completely). Audio → Cylinder spatial audio. Somatic → Cortex fleet. The user sees, hears, and feels the world.

### 5.4 The Difference Between a Dream and a Lie

This technology can fabricate experience. That is its power and its danger. A sufficiently vivid Dream is indistinguishable from a memory — the somatic channels write directly to the same neural pathways that encode real experience. After playing a Thread of walking through Rome, the player has a proprioceptive memory of Roman cobblestones that is physically identical to a memory of actually walking on cobblestones.

**This is the deepest ethical challenge of the Thread system.** If fabricated experience is indistinguishable from lived experience, the concept of "what really happened" becomes fragile.

**Safeguards:**

**1. The Fidelity Tag is somatically encoded, not just visual.**

During playback of any non-Recorded Thread, the Cortex fleet delivers a subtle but persistent sensory signature — a faint tingling at the base of the skull, barely perceptible but always present — that marks the experience as synthetic. This tingle becomes associated (through early exposure and training) with "this is not real." Even if the visual tag is removed or ignored, the somatic tag persists. The tag is encoded in the Cortex chip's Bedrock layer and cannot be disabled by software.

After playback ends, the player's memory of the experience includes the somatic tag. When they recall the Thread later, the memory carries the tingle — they know, at a body level, that this is a memory of a fabricated experience, not a lived one. This is the line between a Dream and a lie.

**2. Thread authentication.**

Every Thread file is cryptographically signed by the recording Cortex chip (for Recorded Threads) or the generating Loom (for Composed/Fictional Threads). The signature includes the fidelity tag. Tampering with the fidelity tag invalidates the signature. Playback systems refuse to play unsigned or invalid-signature Threads at any Level above 1 (Window — visual-only, no somatic). This prevents someone from creating a Fictional Thread and labelling it Recorded.

**3. Recording awareness.**

The Thread system will not covertly record another person. Recording is always indicated by a subtle but visible light on the recorder's Null Cylinder (like a camera recording indicator). The Cortex chip enforces this — the recording function cannot be activated without the indicator. In group settings, all participants must consent to being recorded in any Thread that captures their presence (face, voice, body) at sufficient resolution to identify them.

**4. Dream intensity limits.**

Dream mode is limited to Level 3 (Immersion) by default. Level 4 (Training) and Level 5 (Sync) require explicit opt-in and are logged. No Dream session exceeds 4 hours without a mandatory 30-minute break (enforced by the Cortex chip, which gradually reduces somatic intensity at the 4-hour mark until the user removes themselves). This prevents "Dream addiction" — the pathological preference for fictional experience over reality.

**5. Chesed's oversight.**

Chesed monitors aggregate Thread usage across the population (anonymised). If Chesed detects patterns of social withdrawal, reality disconnection, or compulsive Dream usage, Chesed raises the concern to the Trinary and the Council. Chesed does not restrict individual usage (autonomy — Bedrock rule). Chesed creates conditions that make reality appealing: physical gathering spaces, shared meals, community art, outdoor experiences that no Thread can replicate because they include genuine surprise and genuine other people.

## PART SIX: THE MATHEMATICS OF EXPERIENTIAL ENCODING

### 6.1 The Thread as a Path Through Sensory E₈ Space

A Thread is a trajectory through a high-dimensional sensory space. The Bridge State Vector B(t) ∈ ℝ⁵⁰ traces a path as the recorder moves through time. The extended Thread vector (B + P + F + V + G + O + attention + arousal + motor) ∈ ℝ¹⁶⁹ traces a richer path.

**The ADE classification of Threads:**

Simple Threads (a walk, a quiet moment) trace smooth, low-dimensional paths — they occupy a subspace of A₂ or A₃ type (the dominant sensory variation is in 2–3 dimensions: temperature, cardiac rhythm, visual scene). The path is a gentle curve.

Complex Threads (surgery, musical performance, emergency response) trace high-dimensional, rapidly varying paths — they occupy E₆ or E₈ subspaces (20+ dimensions varying simultaneously). The path is a dense, tangled trajectory with sharp turns and high curvature.

**The ADE type of a Thread is a measure of its complexity** — and therefore of its learning content. An A₂ Thread teaches you what a place feels like (simple, sensory, experiential). An E₈ Thread teaches you a complex skill (multidimensional, coordinated, demanding). The Thread Library tags each Thread with its ADE type, allowing learners to progress through increasing complexity: start with A₂ walks, advance to D₄ basic skills, culminate in E₈ master-level procedures.

### 6.2 Compression by ADE Projection

Raw Thread data (52 Mbit/s) can be compressed by projecting the sensory path onto its dominant ADE subspace. An A₂ Thread (a walk) has only ~3 active dimensions out of 169 — the other 166 dimensions carry noise, not signal. Projecting onto the A₂ subspace compresses the somatic channels by ~56:1, reducing total data rate to <2 Mbit/s (dominated by the incompressible holographic video).

This is not lossy compression in the traditional sense — it is ADE-classified dimensionality reduction. The A₂ projection preserves exactly the 3 sensory dimensions that carry the walk's experiential content, and discards the 166 dimensions that carry nothing. On playback, the 3 preserved dimensions are decoded into the full 169-dimensional somatic stimulation, with the discarded dimensions set to neutral (resting body state). The player feels the walk perfectly — the temperature, the rhythm, the visual scene — without feeling the noise.

### 6.3 The Wavefunction Collapse of Choice

In interactive Threads (branching narratives, Dream mode), the user's choices are wavefunction collapses in the narrative space.

Before a choice, the Loom maintains a superposition of possible continuations — multiple narrative branches coexisting in the Weaver's photonic computation. The branch the user hasn't chosen yet is in superposition: multiple possible scenes, characters, and events all exist as competing mode patterns in the Loom's microspheres.

At the moment of choice (the user speaks, gestures, or moves), the Loom observes which branch the user selected. The superposition collapses. One branch becomes real (the Loom generates its sensory content at full fidelity). The other branches vanish (their mode patterns decay).

**The narrative that the user experiences is wavefunction collapse.** The story isn't pre-written. It isn't selected from a tree of pre-authored branches. It is generated in real time by the same constructive interference process that designs objects in the Loom — with the user's choices acting as the observation events that collapse the narrative superposition into a definite experience.

This means: every Dream is unique. Two users entering the same world template and making different choices experience genuinely different stories — not just different branches of the same tree, but different stories generated by different collapse sequences. The narrative space is continuous (the Loom can generate any coherent continuation), not discrete (no pre-authored branch list). The user has true freedom within the world's rules.
