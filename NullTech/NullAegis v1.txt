## PART TWO: PHYSICAL DESIGN

### 2.1 Philosophy: Second Skin, Not Tank

The Aegis is not bulky power armour. It is a close-fitting suit — 4–8 mm total thickness at most points, 15 mm at the boots, back plate, and forearms where heavier subsystems are housed. From the outside, it looks like a sleek bodysuit with subtle segmentation at the joints, matte ceramic surface in the user's chosen colour, and a smooth full-face visor that can go transparent or opaque.

Total mass: 12 kg (for reference: a heavy winter coat is ~3 kg; a loaded backpack is ~15 kg; military body armour is ~15 kg). The Aegis distributes its mass evenly across the body, so it feels like wearing ~2 kg on each limb and ~4 kg on the torso — noticeable but not burdensome. The powered joint assists (see Section 2.4) compensate for the suit's own mass, so the wearer's net experience is: no heavier than normal clothing, but stronger and more protected.

### 2.2 Suit Architecture: Seven Layers

The Aegis is built from seven concentric layers, innermost to outermost:

**Layer 1 — Comfort Liner (0.5 mm)**

A moisture-wicking, antibacterial, stretch-knit fabric (silver-nanoparticle-infused nylon, same conductive fabric family as the Null Glove). Sits directly against skin. Functions:
- Wicks sweat away from skin, maintaining comfort
- Conductive traces form a full-body sensor mesh (256 contact points measuring skin temperature, galvanic skin response, and mechanical contact pressure) — this is the suit's "touch" sense, detecting how the wearer's body is interacting with the suit at every point
- Provides the radar-reflective patterns (same as Null Glove) for precision body tracking — every joint, every finger segment, every spinal vertebra is tracked by the suit's internal radar at 0.1 mm accuracy and 240 Hz

**Layer 2 — Thermal Regulation (1 mm)**

A network of micro-tubing (0.3 mm diameter, woven into a mesh) carrying circulating thermoregulatory fluid (water-glycol). A micro-pump (in the back plate) circulates the fluid through heating zones (resistive elements in the tubing wall) and cooling zones (thermoelectric Peltier junctions at the back plate, rejecting heat to the outer surface). The system maintains any body-surface temperature from 15°C to 40°C, independently controllable in 16 body zones.

The thermal system cooperates with the Null Cortex fleet: the Cortex reports core body temperature, peripheral vasoconstriction/vasodilation, and metabolic heat generation. The suit adjusts proactively — warming the extremities before the wearer feels cold, cooling the core before they overheat. In extreme environments (−40°C Arctic, +50°C desert, vacuum of space), the thermal system is the primary life-support layer.

**Layer 3 — Structural Mesh (1.5 mm)**

A lattice of high-strength carbon fibre tows (T1100, tensile strength 7 GPa) woven in a triaxial braid pattern (ADE classification: A₂ — the triangular lattice, the strongest 2D structural topology). The mesh provides:

- **Impact protection:** The triaxial braid distributes point loads across a wide area. A 50 J impact (equivalent to a hard punch or a 2 m fall onto a hard surface) is spread over ~200 cm² — the wearer feels a firm push, not a blow. The suit achieves Level IIIA ballistic resistance (stops handgun rounds up to .44 Magnum) at critical zones (chest, back, head) where the mesh is doubled.

- **Cut and puncture resistance:** The carbon fibre tows are extremely difficult to cut (harder than steel at equivalent thickness). The suit resists knife slashes and most punctures.

- **Structural integration:** The mesh is the suit's skeleton — all other layers are bonded to it. It carries the mechanical loads from the joint actuators, distributes the forces from the levitation system, and maintains the suit's shape under dynamic loading.

**Layer 4 — The Photonic Layer (1 mm)**

The suit's brain and primary functional surface. Identical in principle to the Shell Panel's photonic layer, but flexible and body-conforming.

Contents distributed across the full suit surface:
- **64,000 silica microspheres** (R = 15 μm, Q = 10⁸) in a flexible polyimide waveguide mesh. These are smaller than Shell Panel microspheres (15 μm vs 25 μm) to allow the mesh to flex at body-joint radii without damaging the spheres.
- **128 CaF₂ processor microspheres** in 16 E₈ stacks, distributed: 4 in the helmet, 4 in the torso, 2 in each arm, 2 in each leg. The suit has 16× the Null Cylinder's processing power.
- **1,024 VCSELs** (850 nm) for communication with the wearer's Cortex fleet, with adjacent people's Cortex fleets (Bridge), and with environmental systems (Shell rooms, other Aegis suits, vehicles, infrastructure).
- **256 photodiodes** for receiving optical signals.
- **Sensor integration nodes** connecting to Layer 1's touch sensors, Layer 2's thermal sensors, and the suit's external sensors (Layer 6).

**Layer 5 — Actuation Layer (1 mm at joints, 0 elsewhere)**

Artificial muscle fibres — coiled nylon actuators (thermally driven, based on Haines et al., Science 2014) augmented by shape-memory alloy (SMA) wires (NiTi, aka Nitinol). These are placed only at the major joints (shoulders, elbows, wrists, hips, knees, ankles, spine) and at the fingers.

Each joint has:
- 4–8 actuator bundles (agonist/antagonist pairs, like biological muscles)
- Total force per joint: 50–200 N (depending on joint size) — enough to augment the wearer's strength by ~50% (a person who can lift 50 kg can lift 75 kg in the Aegis)
- Response time: 50 ms (SMA fast-response fibres for rapid movements; coiled nylon for sustained force)
- The actuators are driven by the suit's processor via the null billiard control loop — the same D₄ orientation-aware ADE neural network that controls the Null Hover's 16 coils, here controlling 48 joint actuators. The suit predicts the wearer's intended movement (from Layer 1's proprioceptive sensors + Cortex motor planning data) and assists before the wearer's own muscles fully engage.

**This is not an exoskeleton that moves FOR you. It is an exo-musculature that moves WITH you.** The suit's actuators amplify your intent, not replace it. You move naturally; the suit makes every movement 50% easier. Carrying a heavy load feels 50% lighter. Walking uphill feels like walking on flat ground. Reaching overhead feels effortless.

**Layer 6 — Exterior Shell (1 mm)**

The outermost surface. A segmented array of rigid ceramic-coated titanium scales (each ~20 × 20 mm, 0.5 mm thick) overlapping like fish scales or medieval lamellar armour. The scales flex relative to each other at the joints but resist inward deformation — providing the outer hardshell impact resistance.

Each scale incorporates:
- **Electrochromic surface:** Colour and reflectance programmable per-scale. The suit can display any colour, pattern, or image on its surface. Active camouflage (matching the background), high-visibility emergency markings, personal aesthetic expression, or team/organisation identification — all programmable in real time.
- **Microsphere optical emitter:** A cluster of 5 microspheres per scale (5 × ~5,000 scales ≈ 25,000 microspheres), dedicated to the suit's external holographic projection. The suit's outer surface is a 360° holographic display — the same principle as the Null Cylinder but wrapped around a human body. The suit projects holographic imagery into the space around the wearer: information displays, artistic effects, virtual tools, or (in Thread Dream mode) the visual component of an immersive environment.
- **Environmental sensors:** Every 10th scale (500 total) carries a MEMS sensor (alternating: temperature, humidity, barometric pressure, UV intensity, radiation dosimeter, chemical sniffer). The suit has 500 environmental sensors uniformly distributed across its surface, providing a complete environmental map around the wearer's body.

**Layer 7 — Outer Coating (0.01 mm)**

A nanostructured surface treatment applied to the scales:
- **Superhydrophobic:** Water beads off instantly (lotus effect, contact angle >150°). Rain, mud, blood, chemicals sheet off the suit without wetting.
- **Anti-biofouling:** Prevents bacterial adhesion (zwitterionic polymer surface, same as the Null Cortex implant coating). The suit stays clean in biological environments.
- **Low-friction:** Coefficient of friction <0.1. The suit slides against surfaces rather than catching — important for moving through rubble, tight spaces, or underwater.
- **Self-cleaning:** Photocatalytic TiO₂ nanoparticles break down organic contaminants under UV exposure. Walk through sunlight and the suit cleans itself.

## PART FIVE: THE MATHEMATICS — THE BODY AS A BILLIARD DOMAIN

### 5.1 The Body Billiard

The Aegis wraps a photonic computing surface around a human body. The body's shape — the torso cylinder, the limb cylinders, the spherical helmet — defines a billiard domain for the photonic modes in Layer 4.

**The body as a computational geometry:**

A human body, approximated as a collection of cylinders and a sphere, has a surface area of ~1.7 m² (Du Bois formula for average adult). The 64,000 microspheres in Layer 4, at 15 μm radius each, occupy ~0.05 m² of surface — 3% of the body surface. The remaining 97% is waveguide mesh connecting the microspheres.

The waveguide mesh topology follows the body's surface curvature. On the flat torso, the mesh is approximately the A₂ hexagonal lattice (same as Shell Panels). On the curved limbs, the mesh wraps helically — the natural tiling of a cylinder is a helical strip, which maps to a spiral A₂ lattice.

**The joint problem:** At a joint (elbow, knee, shoulder), the mesh must flex without breaking waveguide continuity. The suit uses an accordion-fold waveguide at each joint — a serpentine path that can stretch and compress as the joint articulates, maintaining optical continuity across ±120° of joint angle. Each accordion fold adds ~0.5 mm thickness (contributing to the 1.5 mm total at Layer 5 joints) but preserves photonic coupling.

**The distributed computation exploits the body's geometry.** A computation that requires data from both hands (bimanual coordination — welding, surgery, playing piano) routes photons through the arm waveguides, across the torso mesh, and down the other arm. The signal traversal time (at c/n ≈ 2 × 10⁸ m/s through Si₃N₄ waveguide) for a 2 m path is ~10 ns. The suit's billiard dynamics are faster than nerve impulse speed (~100 m/s in myelinated nerves → 20 ms for the same 2 m path). The suit's photonic computation at the effector (the hand) is 2,000× faster than the biological nerve signal from the brain.

This means: the suit can compute the optimal actuator assistance at the hand before the brain's motor command arrives at the hand's muscles. The suit predicts (from the motor planning cortical signal, read by the Cortex) and pre-actuates. The wearer's hands move with a fluency and precision that exceeds their unaided capability — not because the suit is faster (the wearer initiates the movement) but because the suit's assistance is ready before it's needed.

### 5.2 The Strength Amplification Geometry

The suit's joint actuators amplify force along the ADE-classified kinematic chains of the body:

The human arm, from shoulder to fingertip, is a serial kinematic chain: 7 degrees of freedom (shoulder 3 + elbow 1 + forearm rotation 1 + wrist 2). Each joint is an A₁ link. The chain is an A₆ system (7 links in series — the A₆ Dynkin diagram).

The suit's actuator arrangement mirrors this: 4 actuator bundles at the shoulder (covering the 3 DOF with one redundant), 2 at the elbow (flexion/extension + pronation/supination), 2 at the wrist (flexion/extension + radial/ulnar deviation). Total arm actuators: 8 bundles × 2 arms = 16.

The force amplification at the hand (the end effector) depends on the entire chain's configuration — the same mathematics as robot forward kinematics:

F_hand = J(θ)⁻ᵀ × τ_actuator

where J(θ) is the Jacobian matrix of the arm's kinematic chain at configuration θ (the current joint angles), and τ_actuator is the vector of actuator torques. The suit's processor computes J(θ) in real time (it knows θ from Layer 1's joint tracking) and inverts it to find the actuator torques that produce the desired hand force.

This is a D₄ computation (4-dimensional orientation space — the quaternionic representation of the arm's configuration), running at 10 kHz update rate. The same ADE-classified control that stabilises the Null Hover's 16 coils stabilises the suit's 48 joint actuators.
