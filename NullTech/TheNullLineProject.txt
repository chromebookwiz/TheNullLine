## I. THE EQUATION

Everything in this document traces back to a single mathematical statement:

**k · k = 0**

A photon's four-momentum is null. It has no mass, no rest frame, no proper time. But confined inside a reflective boundary, null rays produce mass (K·K = −q²), time (τ > 0 at the vertex), information (ADE-classified periodic orbits), and computation (the null billiard dynamics that transform input states into output states through wavefunction collapse).

The Null Line Project is the systematic exploitation of this equation across every domain of human activity: computation, communication, medicine, fabrication, transportation, exploration, governance, and the expansion of civilisation beyond Earth.

## III. THE CRITICAL BREAKTHROUGHS

The timeline assumes specific breakthroughs in photonic computing that accelerate the entire programme. Each breakthrough has a current state, a required advance, and an estimated timeline.

### Breakthrough 1: Ultra-High-Q Microsphere Fabrication

**Current state (2026):** Fused silica microspheres with Q ≈ 10⁹, fabricated by CO₂ laser reflow of optical fibre tips. Surface roughness ~0.3 nm RMS. Diameter control ±2 μm.

**Required advance:** Q > 10¹¹ in CaF₂, with sub-nanometre diameter control and atomically smooth surfaces. This enables: millisecond photon lifetimes (long-term memory), trillion-reflection billiard orbits (deep computation), and Riemann-zero-tuned mode spectra (fundamental physics experiments).

**Pathway:** This is a fabrication problem, not a physics problem. CaF₂ Q > 10¹¹ has been demonstrated in mm-scale discs by diamond-turning. Scaling to 100 μm spheres requires either: (a) precision CNC diamond turning at the microsphere scale, or (b) the Null Forge itself (bootstrapping problem — see timeline).

**Estimated breakthrough: Year 2–4.** Early Forges use conventionally fabricated silica spheres (Q ~ 10⁹). These Forges fabricate better CaF₂ spheres (Q ~ 10¹¹). The better spheres go into the next generation of processors and Forges. The bootstrap completes in 2 generations.

### Breakthrough 2: Integrated Photonic Phase Control

**Current state (2026):** LiNbO₃-on-insulator (LNOI) modulators can switch at >100 GHz with V_π < 2 V. Integration density: ~1,000 modulators per chip (HyperLight, 2024). VCSEL arrays: ~10,000 emitters demonstrated.

**Required advance:** 4,096+ individually phase-controlled VCSELs in a 28 mm circumference (for the Null Cylinder) or 2 million VCSELs in a 3.5 m dome (for the Null Disc canopy). Each paired with a LiNbO₃ modulator, all driven by a null sphere processor computing holographic phase patterns at 120 Hz.

**Pathway:** VCSEL density is limited by thermal management (each VCSEL dissipates ~1 mW). At 4,096 VCSELs in 28 mm circumference: ~4 W total in a 28 mm ring — manageable with passive heatsinking. At 2 million VCSELs in 3.5 m: ~2 kW — requires active cooling but is within standard thermal engineering. The integration challenge is photolithographic: patterning 4,096 VCSEL/modulator pairs on a curved surface. This is a packaging problem, solvable with existing semiconductor tooling adapted for curved substrates.

**Estimated breakthrough: Year 3–5.** The Null Cylinder's display is the forcing function. First prototypes use fewer VCSELs (1,024) with lower holographic resolution, upgrading as integration density improves.

### Breakthrough 3: Room-Temperature Single-Photon Nonlinearity

**Current state (2026):** Purdue's single-photon transistor (Nature Nanotechnology, 2025) demonstrates photon-photon interaction at room temperature in CMOS-compatible silicon. This is the CAPTURE gate — the nonlinear null combination that makes photonic computation possible.

**Required advance:** Integration of single-photon nonlinear elements with WGM microspheres. The CAPTURE gate must operate inside (or coupled to) the microsphere, not as a separate chip. Needed for: on-sphere computation, intra-sphere mode mixing, and the Weaver's constructive interference design.

**Pathway:** Coat the microsphere equator with a thin strip (~1 μm wide) of the Purdue photon-transistor material (doped silicon avalanche region). Photons in WGM modes passing through this strip interact nonlinearly. Alternatively, use the microsphere's intrinsic Kerr nonlinearity (four-wave mixing), which is weaker but universal and already demonstrated in WGM resonators.

**Estimated breakthrough: Year 2–3.** The Kerr approach works now (demonstrated). The single-photon transistor integration requires ~2 years of materials engineering. Both paths are pursued in parallel.

### Breakthrough 4: Null Sphere Processor First-Light

**Current state (2026):** All components exist separately. WGM microspheres: demonstrated. Evanescent coupling between spheres: demonstrated. Four-wave mixing in WGM: demonstrated. Si₃N₄ waveguide buses: commercial. No one has assembled a multi-sphere processor that computes in the null billiard framework.

**Required advance:** Demonstrate that 8 coupled microspheres in E₈ topology perform a computation — any computation — by injecting photons, allowing billiard dynamics, and observing the output.

**Pathway:** This is the first integration milestone. Assemble 8 silica spheres (Q ~ 10⁸, conventionally fabricated) on a silicon interposer with Si₃N₄ waveguide coupling. Inject two WGM modes, observe four-wave mixing products, verify that the output matches the null billiard prediction (Theorem 2.1).

**Estimated breakthrough: Year 1–2.** This is the single most important near-term milestone. Everything else waits for this.

### Breakthrough 5: Sustainable D-³He Fusion

**Current state (2026):** D-T fusion approaching breakeven (ITER first plasma expected ~2035, NIF demonstrated ignition 2022). D-³He has been demonstrated in laboratory experiments (Wisconsin IEC, PPPL) but not at breakeven. ³He supply is limited to ~15 kg/year on Earth; lunar regolith contains ~1 million tonnes.

**Required advance:** Sustained D-³He burn with energy gain Q > 10 in a compact reactor suitable for spacecraft installation. Plus: lunar ³He extraction at industrial scale.

**Pathway:** This is the longest-pole item in the interstellar programme. D-³He requires higher plasma temperatures than D-T (the Coulomb barrier is higher), necessitating either: (a) advanced magnetic confinement (compact stellarator or field-reversed configuration), or (b) inertial confinement with photonic ignition (using the Null Forge-fabricated precision targets). Lunar ³He extraction requires a permanent lunar mining operation — which requires the Forge (for fabricating extraction equipment) and the Hover (for surface transport).

**Estimated breakthrough: Year 20–35.** This is the pacing item for the Null Ark and Seedship fleet.
